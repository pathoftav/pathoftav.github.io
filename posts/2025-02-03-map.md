# The Map Is a Territory

We repeat that the map is not the territory as if it settled something. But a map, once drawn, becomes a territory of its own — with borders, disputed regions, and inhabitants who have never seen the land it claims to describe.

Most arguments about metaphysics are border disputes between maps.

## Borders

Every map begins by deciding what it will not show. The decision is the map.

## Inhabitants

| Map | Native population |
| --- | ----------------- |
| Aristotle's cosmos | Unmoved movers |
| Ptolemy's spheres | Intelligences |
| The Tree | Everything, eventually |

## Disputes

When two maps disagree, the argument is rarely about the land.
